Question 1 of 2 General Knowledge / Descriptive
"Where do people in your country often go for holidays?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • Describe popular domestic and international holiday destinations.
  • Explain why these destinations are popular.
  • Mention any trends in holiday preferences.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Domestic destinations like Da Nang, Phu Quoc, and Ha Long Bay are extremely popular for beach
  • holidays. International destinations like Thailand, South Korea, and Japan have become increasingly popular as air travel has become more affordable.
  • City breaks to Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi attract both domestic and international tourists.
  • There is a growing trend towards eco-tourism and adventure travel among younger Vietnamese

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Vietnamese people have a wide range of holiday preferences. For domestic travel, beach destinations are enormously popular - places like Da Nang, Phu Quoc, and Nha Trang attract millions of visitors every year, particularly during the summer months. Ha Long Bay is another iconic destination that draws both Vietnamese and international tourists. For international travel, Thailand has traditionally been the most popular destination due to its proximity and affordability, but South Korea and Japan have become increasingly popular in recent years, driven partly by the influence of Korean and Japanese pop culture. Among younger travellers, there is a growing interest in eco-tourism, adventure travel, and off-the-beaten- track destinations that offer more authentic cultural experiences.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion
"Is it important to plan a holiday?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • State your opinion and give reasons.
  • Acknowledge the value of spontaneity.
  • Consider different types of travellers and trips.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Planning ensures you make the most of limited time and budget, particularly for international travel.
  • Booking accommodation and transport in advance can save significant money.
  • However, over-planning can remove the spontaneity and serendipity that make travel memorable.
  • The ideal approach depends on the type of trip and the traveller's personality.
🔗 Key Vocabulary
plan aheadbook in advancemake the most ofleave room fordeal with (unexpected situations)carry ontravel planningitineraryspontaneous travelbudget managementtravel experience
✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

I think some degree of planning is important, particularly for international travel where logistics are more complex and the consequences of poor planning - missed flights, no accommodation, budget overruns - can be significant. Booking key elements like flights and accommodation in advance typically saves money and reduces stress. However, I am also a firm believer in leaving room for spontaneity. Some of the most memorable travel experiences I have had were completely unplanned - stumbling upon a local festival, following a recommendation from a stranger, or simply wandering without a destination in mind. I think the ideal approach is to plan the essentials - transport, accommodation, and one or two must-see experiences - while leaving the rest of the itinerary flexible enough to respond to whatever you discover along the way.
