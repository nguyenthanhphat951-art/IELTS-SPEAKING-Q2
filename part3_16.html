✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Effective employee encouragement requires a multifaceted approach, because different people are motivated by different things. That said, some strategies are broadly effective. Recognition is perhaps the most powerful: publicly acknowledging good work, celebrating achievements, and expressing genuine appreciation costs nothing but has an enormous impact on morale and motivation. Providing opportunities for professional development - training, mentorship, challenging projects, and clear career progression pathways - shows employees that the organisation is invested in their growth, which builds loyalty and engagement. Creating a positive, inclusive work culture where people feel psychologically safe to share ideas and make mistakes without fear of punishment is also crucial. And of course, competitive compensation and benefits, including flexible working arrangements, are important baseline factors. The most effective leaders understand that motivation is personal and take the time to understand what drives each individual member of their team.

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Yes, undoubtedly. Some people are significantly more persuasive than others, and I think this reflects a combination of natural aptitude and developed skill. The most persuasive people tend to share certain qualities: they are excellent listeners who genuinely understand the perspectives and concerns of the people they are trying to persuade; they communicate with clarity, confidence, and appropriate emotion; and they are skilled at framing their arguments in ways that resonate with their specific audience. They also tend to have high emotional intelligence, which allows them to read the room and adjust their approach accordingly. That said, I do not think persuasion is purely a natural talent - it is very much a learnable skill. With practice, study, and honest self-reflection, most people can significantly improve their ability to communicate persuasively. The important caveat is that ethical persuasion relies on honest argument and genuine understanding, not manipulation or deception.
