Question 1 of 2 Opinion
"Is it good to arrive early in any situation?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • State your opinion and give reasons.
  • Mention situations where arriving early is particularly important.
  • Acknowledge situations where it might not matter.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Arriving early demonstrates respect for other people's time and shows professionalism.
  • ●
  • The importance of punctuality varies significantly across cultures.
🔗 Key Vocabulary
show up earlyget there ahead of timemake a good impressiondeal with (unexpected delays)carry onpunctualitytime managementprofessional etiquettecultural normfirst impression
✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Generally speaking, yes - arriving early is a positive habit that demonstrates respect for other people's time and shows a level of professionalism and reliability that is universally valued. In professional contexts like job interviews, meetings, or presentations, arriving early gives you time to compose yourself, review your preparation, and make a calm, confident first impression. It also provides a buffer against unexpected delays. However, I think it is worth acknowledging that the importance of punctuality varies across cultures and contexts. In some social settings, arriving exactly on time or even slightly late is perfectly acceptable or even expected. And in very informal situations among close friends, strict punctuality is rarely a concern. The key is to read the context and adjust accordingly.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion / Reason
"Why do some people like to stay up late?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy

  • Mention the concept of chronotypes.
  • Avoid being judgemental about night owls.

  • The evening offers quiet, uninterrupted time that is difficult to find during the busy daytime hours.
  • Social activities, entertainment, and online communities are often more active in the evening.
  • Some people stay up late due to work demands, particularly those in creative or digital fields.
🔗 Key Vocabulary

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

There are several reasons, and I think it is important not to assume that staying up late is simply a bad habit. From a biological perspective, people have different chronotypes - some are naturally more alert and productive in the morning, while others genuinely function better in the evening. For natural night owls, staying up late is not a choice so much as a reflection of their biology. Beyond biology, the evening often offers something that the daytime does not: quiet, uninterrupted time. For people with busy, demanding days, the late evening may be the only time they have to pursue personal projects, creative work, or simply to decompress. Social factors also play a role - many social activities, entertainment options, and online communities are more active in the evening. The challenge, of course, is that most societal structures - work schedules, school hours - are designed around morning activity, which can make life difficult for committed night owls.
