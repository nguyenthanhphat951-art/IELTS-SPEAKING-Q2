Question 1 of 3 Opinion
"Do you think car colours are important?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
stand outblend ingo foropt for
💡 Collocations
resale valuepersonal tastecolour preferencesafety visibilityneutral colour
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

To some extent, yes. I think colour matters both practically and personally. Neutral colours like white or silver tend to have better resale value because they appeal to a wider range of buyers. On a personal level, though, colour is a form of self-expression - some people go for bold reds or blues to reflect their personality. From a safety standpoint, brighter colours can also improve visibility on the road, which is a practical consideration many people overlook.

Those car journeys felt like adventures, and they are some of my fondest childhood memories.

Question 3 of 3 Preference
"Do you prefer to be a driver or a passenger?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
take the wheelsit backrely onfocus on
💡 Collocations
in controlsense of freedombackseat drivernavigate trafficenjoy the ride
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

I would say I prefer being a passenger, honestly. When someone else is driving, I can sit back, look out the window, and actually enjoy the scenery without having to focus on navigating traffic. I find driving in busy city conditions quite stressful, so being a passenger lets me relax and arrive at my destination feeling refreshed. That said, on open roads or during road trips, I do enjoy taking the wheel - there is a real sense of freedom in that.
