✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Yes, definitely. I live in a city that has a number of well-preserved colonial-era buildings from the French colonial period, and I find them absolutely fascinating. One that particularly stands out is the old post office in the city centre - it has a stunning yellow facade, high arched ceilings, and intricate ironwork that feels completely out of place in the modern urban landscape, in the best possible way. Every time I walk past it, I am struck by how much history it represents and how beautifully it has been maintained.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion
"Do you think we should preserve old buildings in cities?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
tear downrestorepreservestand the test of time
💡 Collocations
cultural heritagearchitectural historyurban developmenthistorical preservationmodern
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Absolutely, I believe old buildings should be preserved wherever possible. They are physical links to our history and cultural identity - once demolished, that connection is lost forever. Many old buildings also have remarkable architectural beauty that modern constructions often lack. From an economic perspective, well-preserved historical buildings attract tourists and contribute to local economies. Of course, there are cases where safety concerns or urban development needs make preservation impractical, but in those situations, I think we should at least document and commemorate what is being lost.
