Question 1 of 2 Personal Experience / Habit
"Can you remember the dreams you had?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs

💡 Collocations

✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Sometimes, yes - but it really depends on the dream. If it is particularly vivid or emotionally intense, I tend to remember it quite clearly for a few hours after waking up. But most of my dreams fade away within minutes of getting out of bed. I have noticed that I am more likely to remember dreams when I wake up naturally rather than to an alarm. I occasionally have recurring dreams about being late for an important exam, which I think reflects everyday stress. I have thought about keeping a dream journal, but I have not gotten around to it yet.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion
"Do you think dreams have special meanings?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
make sense oflook intoreflect onattach meaning to
💡 Collocations
subconscious mindpsychological interpretationsymbolic meaningdream analysisinner
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

I am somewhat sceptical, to be honest. From a scientific perspective, dreams are thought to be the brain's way of processing emotions and consolidating memories during sleep, rather than carrying specific prophetic meanings. That said, I do think dreams can reflect our inner anxieties and desires - so in that sense, they might reveal something about our subconscious state of mind. Some cultures attach great spiritual significance to dreams, and I respect that perspective even if I do not personally subscribe to it.
