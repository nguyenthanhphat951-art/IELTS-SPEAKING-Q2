✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Yes, I think plan changes are extremely common - perhaps more so than ever in our fast-paced, unpredictable world. Plans change for all sorts of reasons: unexpected circumstances arise, new information comes to light, priorities shift, or the original plan simply turns out to be impractical. Technology has also made it much easier to change plans at short notice - a quick message or phone call can rearrange an entire schedule in seconds, which may have made people more comfortable with last- minute changes than previous generations were. I think the ability to adapt plans flexibly is generally a positive quality - it reflects resilience and pragmatism rather than unreliability. The key is to communicate changes clearly and considerately to anyone affected, and to approach the new situation with a constructive attitude rather than dwelling on what was lost.

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Important decisions are rarely made through a single, simple process - most people use a combination of approaches. Rational analysis plays an important role: gathering information, identifying options, weighing the pros and cons of each, and considering the likely consequences. But intuition also matters enormously - that gut feeling that something is right or wrong, which often reflects accumulated experience and wisdom that is difficult to articulate explicitly. Most people also seek input from others: trusted friends, family members, mentors, or professionals who can offer perspectives and information that the decision-maker might not have considered. I think the best decision-making processes combine all of these elements: thorough information gathering, honest self-reflection about one's values and priorities, input from trusted others, and ultimately the courage to commit to a choice and take responsibility for it.
