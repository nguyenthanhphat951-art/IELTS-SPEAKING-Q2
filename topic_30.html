✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Honestly, I would not say I am a natural morning person, but I have trained myself to get up early because I find mornings incredibly productive. I usually wake up at around six o'clock, have a glass of water, and spend the first hour reading or exercising before the day gets busy. Once I am up and moving, I actually enjoy the quiet of the early morning - there is a calm before the world wakes up that I find really peaceful. It took some getting used to, but now I genuinely appreciate starting the day early.

Question 2 of 2 Comparison (Past vs. Present)
"Are there any differences between what you do in the morning now and what you did in the"

💡 Collocations

✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Yes, quite a few differences, actually. When I was a student, my mornings were quite chaotic - I would often sleep until the last possible minute, rush to get ready, and skip breakfast entirely. Now, I have a much more structured morning routine. I wake up at a consistent time, exercise, have a proper breakfast, and review my plans for the day before leaving home. The change came gradually as I started to notice how much better I felt and performed when I had a calm, organised start to the day. I think morning routines are one of the most underrated factors in overall well-being.
