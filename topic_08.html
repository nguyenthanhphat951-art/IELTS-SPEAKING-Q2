Question 1 of 3 Preference
"What kind of clothes do you like to wear?"

💡 Collocations

✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

I tend to gravitate towards smart casual clothing - things like well-fitted jeans, plain T-shirts, and simple button-down shirts. I prioritise comfort over fashion, so I rarely wear anything that feels restrictive or impractical. For work or formal occasions, I will dress up a bit more, but even then I prefer clean, minimalist styles over anything too flashy. I think good clothing does not have to be expensive - it just needs to fit well and suit the occasion.

Question 2 of 3 Preference
"Do you prefer to wear comfortable and casual clothes or smart clothes?"

💡 Collocations

✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

I definitely lean towards comfortable, casual clothes in my everyday life. I find that when I am physically comfortable in what I am wearing, I am more relaxed and productive. That said, I do appreciate the confidence boost that comes from dressing smartly for important occasions - a job interview, a formal dinner, or a professional presentation. I think the key is knowing when each style is appropriate. For most of my daily activities, though, casual wins every time.

Question 3 of 3 Habit / Opinion
"Do you spend a lot of time choosing clothes?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
pick outtry ondecide ongo with
💡 Collocations
morning routineoutfit planningwardrobe organisationfashion-consciousdecision fatigue
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Not particularly, no. I tend to keep my wardrobe fairly simple and organised, which means getting dressed in the morning is usually a quick process. I typically spend about five minutes choosing an outfit, and I often plan what I will wear the night before to save time in the morning. I think having a capsule wardrobe - a small collection of versatile, well-fitting pieces that all work together - makes the whole process much easier. I admire people who have a strong sense of personal style, but I prefer to keep things simple.
