Question 1 of 3 Past Experience
"When did you start using social media?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs

💡 Collocations

✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

I started using social media when I was around fourteen or fifteen years old. The first platform I signed up for was Facebook, mainly because all my classmates were using it and I did not want to feel left out. Back then, it was mostly about keeping in touch with friends and sharing photos. I gradually got into other platforms like Instagram and later TikTok as they became popular. Looking back, it is remarkable how much social media has evolved since those early days.

Question 2 of 3 Opinion / Self-reflection
"Do you think you spend too much time on social media?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
cut back onget caught up inlog offset limits on
💡 Collocations
screen timedigital detoxmindless scrollingsocial media addictiontime management
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

Honestly, yes - I think I do sometimes spend more time on social media than I should. I would estimate I spend around two to three hours a day scrolling through various platforms, which adds up to a significant chunk of my free time. I have noticed that I often get caught up in mindless scrolling without any real purpose. To address this, I have started setting daily time limits on my phone and trying to log off at least an hour before bed. It is a work in progress, but I am gradually cutting back.

Question 3 of 3 General Knowledge
"What do people often do on social media?"
🔗 Phrasal Verbs
share contentfollow accountsengage withreach out tokeep up with
💡 Collocations
post updatesshare photoswatch videosfollow influencersonline community
✅ Band 7+ Sample Answer

People use social media for a huge range of purposes. The most common activities are sharing photos and personal updates, watching short videos, and keeping up with news and trends. Many people also use it to follow their favourite celebrities, brands, or content creators. Social media is also widely used for communication - messaging friends and family, joining interest-based groups, and even networking professionally on platforms like LinkedIn. On the more passive side, a lot of people simply scroll through their feeds for entertainment, which can easily become a time-consuming habit.
