Question 1 of 3 Opinion / Analysis
"Are rivers and lakes important to a country?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Rivers and lakes are absolutely vital to a country's well-being across multiple dimensions. Most fundamentally, they are critical sources of fresh water for drinking, agriculture, and industry - without reliable access to fresh water, human civilisation as we know it would not be possible. Ecologically, rivers and lakes support extraordinarily rich and diverse ecosystems, providing habitat for countless species of fish, birds, and other wildlife. Economically, they support fishing industries, agriculture, hydroelectric power generation, and in many cases, tourism. Historically, the world's great civilisations - Egyptian, Mesopotamian, Chinese, Indian - all developed along major river systems, and waterways remain important for transport and trade in many parts of the world. And culturally, rivers and lakes often hold deep spiritual and symbolic significance, featuring prominently in the myths, religions, and artistic traditions of the societies that live alongside them.

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Absolutely, I think parents have a crucial role to play in environmental education, and arguably the most important one. Parents are children's first and most influential teachers, and the values and habits instilled in early childhood tend to persist throughout life. A parent who models environmentally conscious behaviour - recycling, reducing waste, choosing sustainable products, spending time in nature - teaches their child far more effectively than any classroom lesson. Parents can also make environmental education practical and tangible: taking children to parks and natural areas, explaining why we should not litter, involving them in composting or growing vegetables. That said, I do not think parental education alone is sufficient. Schools, governments, and communities all have important roles to play in creating a culture of environmental responsibility. The most effective approach is one where these different influences reinforce each other.

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

There are meaningful actions at every level. At the individual level, the most impactful things people can do are reducing meat consumption - particularly beef, which has an enormous carbon footprint - cutting down on single-use plastics, reducing energy consumption at home, and choosing more sustainable transport options like walking, cycling, or public transport. At the community level, people can participate in local conservation initiatives, support businesses with strong environmental credentials, and advocate for better environmental policies from local governments. At the systemic level, perhaps the most important thing individuals can do is use their voice and their vote to support politicians and policies that take environmental protection seriously. I think it is important to acknowledge that individual action, while valuable, is insufficient on its own - the scale of the environmental challenges we face requires systemic change driven by policy, regulation, and corporate accountability.
