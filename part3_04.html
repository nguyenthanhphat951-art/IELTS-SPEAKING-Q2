Question 1 of 3 Opinion / Analysis
"What are the advantages and disadvantages of advertisements?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • Present a balanced view with specific examples.
  • Consider economic, social, and psychological dimensions.
  • Use structured language for clarity.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Advantage: advertising informs consumers about products and services, enabling more informed
  • Advantage: advertising funds free media content - without advertising revenue, many websites, TV
  • Disadvantage: advertising can be manipulative, exploiting psychological vulnerabilities to create
  • Disadvantage: advertising contributes to overconsumption and materialism, encouraging people to buy

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Advertising has both genuine benefits and significant drawbacks. On the positive side, it serves an important informational function - it tells consumers about products and services that might genuinely improve their lives, and it funds a vast amount of free content that we all enjoy, from websites to television programmes. Without advertising revenue, much of the media landscape as we know it would not exist. On the negative side, advertising is often deliberately manipulative, exploiting psychological vulnerabilities - our insecurities, our desire for status, our fear of missing out - to create artificial desires for things we do not actually need. This contributes to overconsumption, environmental damage, and a culture of materialism that many people find deeply unsatisfying. The challenge is to preserve the legitimate informational and economic functions of advertising while developing stronger ethical standards around its more manipulative practices.

Question 2 of 3 Opinion / Analysis
"Why are many advertisements endorsed by celebrities? How useful are they?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • Explain why companies use celebrity endorsements.
  • Assess their effectiveness from both the company's and consumer's perspective.
  • Mention any potential downsides of celebrity endorsements.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Celebrities bring instant recognition and credibility to a brand, reaching their large and loyal fan bases.
  • Consumers often aspire to emulate celebrities they admire, making them more receptive to products
  • Celebrity endorsements can be very effective for brand awareness but less so for driving actual
  • The risk is that a celebrity's personal scandal can damage the brand they endorse.
🔗 Key Vocabulary
endorse a productappeal tolook up tobe taken in bystand out frombuild up (brand image)celebrity endorsementbrand ambassadorconsumer trustaspirational marketingbrand association
✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Companies use celebrity endorsements primarily because celebrities bring instant recognition, credibility, and access to their enormous fan bases. When a beloved celebrity endorses a product, their fans are more likely to notice the advertisement, trust the product, and consider purchasing it. There is also a powerful aspirational dimension - people who admire a celebrity often want to emulate their lifestyle, and buying the products they endorse feels like a step in that direction. As for how useful they are - the evidence is mixed. Celebrity endorsements are generally very effective at building brand awareness and creating positive associations. However, they are less reliably effective at driving actual sales, particularly for products that require careful consideration. There is also a significant risk: if the celebrity becomes embroiled in a scandal, the brand they endorse can suffer serious reputational damage by association.

Question 3 of 3 Opinion / Comparison
"Which is more effective, online advertising or offline advertising?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • Compare the two types with specific examples.
  • Consider targeting, cost-effectiveness, reach, and measurability.
  • Give a nuanced conclusion rather than a simple answer.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Online advertising offers precise targeting, real-time data, and measurable results at relatively low
  • Offline advertising (TV, billboards, print) reaches broader audiences and can create stronger emotional
  • Online advertising is generally more cost-effective for small businesses with limited budgets.
  • The most effective approach typically combines both, using each for what it does best.
🔗 Key Vocabulary

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

I think the honest answer is that it depends on the goal, the audience, and the budget. Online advertising has some significant advantages: it allows for extraordinarily precise targeting - you can reach specific demographics, interests, and behaviours - and it provides real-time, measurable data on performance. This makes it particularly cost-effective for businesses with limited budgets who need to maximise their return on investment. Offline advertising, on the other hand, excels at building broad brand awareness and creating emotional impact. A well-crafted television commercial or a striking billboard can reach millions of people and create a lasting impression in a way that a digital banner ad rarely achieves. For most businesses, the most effective approach is a thoughtful combination of both - using online channels for targeted, measurable campaigns and offline channels for brand-building and reaching audiences who are less active online.
