✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

The differences are quite pronounced. Young people today tend to gravitate towards fast-paced, visually stimulating content - reality shows, gaming streams, short-form videos on TikTok or YouTube, and serialised dramas with complex plots and frequent cliffhangers. They also consume content primarily on demand through streaming platforms, watching what they want when they want it. Older viewers, on the other hand, often prefer more traditional formats - news programmes, historical dramas, variety shows, and documentaries. They are also more likely to watch scheduled television at fixed times. I think these differences reflect broader generational differences in attention spans, cultural references, and familiarity with technology, rather than any fundamental difference in intelligence or taste. Both groups are simply consuming media in ways that feel natural and comfortable to them.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion / Analysis
"What makes a popular TV or online program?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy
  • ● Give specific examples to illustrate your points.
  • Consider both content quality and marketing/distribution factors.
💡 Ideas to Use in Your Answer
  • Compelling characters that audiences can relate to or be fascinated by are essential.
  • A strong narrative hook that creates suspense and makes viewers want to know what happens next.
  • High production values - good cinematography, sound design, and visual effects - enhance the viewing
  • Effective marketing and availability on popular platforms significantly influence a programme's reach
🔗 Key Vocabulary
get hooked onkeep up withbinge-watchdraw in (viewers)stand out fromcarry on watchingcompelling storylinecharacter developmentproduction qualityaudience engagementstreaming platform
✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

I think several factors combine to make a programme truly popular. The most fundamental is compelling characters - audiences need to care about the people on screen, whether they love them, hate them, or are simply fascinated by them. Without that emotional investment, even the most spectacular production will fail to retain viewers. A strong narrative structure is equally important - the ability to create suspense, surprise, and a sense of forward momentum that makes people want to keep watching. Beyond content, production quality matters enormously in an era when audiences have access to world-class content from Netflix, HBO, and other premium platforms. And finally, distribution and marketing play a crucial role - a brilliant programme that nobody knows about or cannot easily access will struggle to find its audience.
