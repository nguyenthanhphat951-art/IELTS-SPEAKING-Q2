✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

This is a fascinating question that does not have a simple answer. Young children tend to smile most frequently and spontaneously - they experience joy in an uninhibited, immediate way that adults often lose as they become more self-conscious and burdened by responsibility. Adolescents and young adults may actually smile less, as this is often a period of heightened social anxiety, identity uncertainty, and life pressure. Interestingly, research on emotional well-being suggests that older adults - particularly those in their sixties and beyond - often report higher levels of contentment and emotional stability than younger people, which may translate into more genuine, frequent smiling. I think the relationship between age and smiling is ultimately less about age itself and more about life circumstances, personality, and the degree to which a person has found meaning, connection, and contentment in their life.

Question 2 of 2 Opinion / Analysis
"Do women smile more than men? Why?"
🎯 Answer Direction & Strategy

  • Avoid stereotyping - acknowledge individual variation.

  • Men may feel social pressure to appear more stoic and less emotionally expressive.

✅ Band 7+ Model Answer

Research does suggest that women tend to smile more frequently than men on average, but I think it is important to understand why before drawing any conclusions. The difference appears to be largely social and cultural rather than innate. From a very young age, girls are often socialised to be more emotionally expressive, warm, and accommodating - smiling is part of this expected social performance. Boys, on the other hand, are often socialised to be more stoic and emotionally restrained, with smiling sometimes associated with weakness or submissiveness in certain cultural contexts. So the difference in smiling frequency between men and women reflects social conditioning and cultural expectations more than any fundamental difference in happiness or emotional experience. It is also worth noting that there is enormous individual variation within both groups, and that these patterns vary significantly across different cultures.
